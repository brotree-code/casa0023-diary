About Me

Hello everyone, I’m Jianshu Wu. I grew up in Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province in southwest China — a mountainous region renowned for its dramatic karst landscapes, cool summers, and extraordinary cultural diversity. I am Buyi, one of the major ethnic groups of Guizhou, whose culture is woven into the landscape itself: Buyi villages traditionally cluster along rivers and terraced fields, our festivals mark the rhythms of the agricultural calendar, and our hand-woven batik textiles carry patterns that have been passed down for generations. Growing up with that connection between people, land, and season gave me an early intuition that places are never just physical space — they carry layers of identity and meaning that no satellite image can fully capture, but that spatial analysis can at least begin to illuminate.

I completed my undergraduate degree at the School of Information Resource Management, Renmin University of China, where I developed a foundation in data management, information systems, and spatial thinking. That background made the leap into Earth Observation feel like a natural extension — both fields are ultimately about extracting meaning from large, complex datasets.

I chose CASA0023 because I wanted to move beyond working with pre-processed spatial data and understand how the raw signals from satellites are turned into the maps and indices that underpin urban policy. GEE in particular felt like a tool I needed to actually use, not just read about.

Welcome

This is my learning diary for CASA0023: Remotely Sensing Cities and Environments. Each weekly entry follows the same structure: a summary of the key ideas from the lecture and practical, an applications section discussing how those ideas appear in research and policy, and a personal reflection on what I found interesting, what I struggled with, and where the methods might be useful later.

How I’m Working

The workflow evolved across the term. Weeks 1–4 used R / RStudio and QGIS for local processing, moving to Google Earth Engine (JavaScript) from Week 6 onwards — with R returning in Week 9 for post-processing and visualisation alongside GEE. The study areas shifted from my hometown Guiyang, to Beijing, to Ahmedabad, then to Chengdu for the classification weeks, and back to Beijing for the temperature analysis in Week 9. Large EO datasets are not stored in the GitHub repository — only the figures used in each diary entry are committed there.
